me

I ended the summer of 2017 with $120,000 in profit playing daily fantasy sports. I had some good profits from my Ethereum and Bitcoin investments. For nearly 10 years I had earned my living playing Poker and Daily Fantasy Sports online. Though I had my best season ever that summer, the reality was making money gambling was getting harder and harder year by year. I knew that season would be one of my last.

The ability to study my interests without stress or responsibility what I'm most thankful for. I studied statistics, physics, mathematics, finance, investing, game theory, history, cryptocurrency, art, and anything else that interested me. Despite being a physics dropout, watching free university lectures on youtube is among my favorite activities to do in my free time. I never studied anything because I had to, or because I had a career goal. Everything I studied was because it interested me at the time, and I am so lucky to have had the opportunity to do that.

But my most important interest came at the end of that summer in 2017. Increasingly I started turning to technology to give me an extra edge. It started with using Microsoft Excel along with some statistical modeling. During that summer of 2017 I learned VBA for excel to automate my processes and scrape data. I found programming to be pretty interesting as I got better at it, so naturally I kept studying it.

My interest in computers and programming went much further than I could have anticipated. I binged hours of of YouTube videos about computer science. By winter I was playing code golf for fun nearly everyday, learning machine learning, dabbling in web development, and considering turning my obsession into a career.

I feel extremely lucky to have stumbled upon something I am so interested in. I enjoyed tons of subjects I have studied over the years but none of them turned into an obsession like programming did. As soon as I wake up everyday I want to start programming or learning more about programming. On March 5th 2018 I start a coding bootcamp to hopefully kick off a career as a software developer.

this blog

This blog is about sharing anything I am learning that is interesting. It is mostly focused on technology, but can veer off into many different directions from time to time. Please connect with me on Twitter or contact anytime to have a conversation.
